Rebuilding Kyra's performance system from a politeness machine into a signal engine.

Series A, ~60 people, ~$25M ARR. Inherited a JD-based biannual review. Looked healthy on paper. Wasn't.

DiagnosisThree faults in the legacy system

The review wasn't broken in execution.
It was broken in design.

05 / 12
01 · Anchor

Anchored to the JD, not to impact.

Performance was scored against role descriptions written at hire. People rated themselves "doing the job" — which they were. Commercial outcomes never entered the conversation.

02 · Cadence

Twice a year. Then forgotten.

Six-month gaps between signal and consequence. Managers couldn't remember February by August. Ratings drifted toward the mean by default.

03 · Distribution

No calibration. No real curve.

Every manager rated their own. Without cross-team calibration, ratings inflated and comp drifted off signal. Talent density was invisible.

The rebuildImpact Factor v2.0
The rebuild · Impact Factor v2.0

From a politeness machine to a signal engine.

"Score the outcome, not the activity. Then calibrate it together, in the open, on a power curve — and let comp follow signal."
What changed

Impact Factor v2.0

  • Role-specific commercial metrics replaced JD-adherence as the scoring anchor.
  • Distribution moved to a power curve — most people cluster mid; outliers earn outsized recognition.
  • Quarterly calibration with cross-team managers, recorded with structured evidence.
  • Comp recommendations generated from rating × Impact Factor × tenure-adjusted band.
  • Growth conversations split into enrichment vs enlargement tracks.
The toolkitFour frameworks I carry across roles

The four frameworks that hold the whole system together.

07 / 12
01 · Impact Factor

Score impact, not activity.

Role-specific commercial metrics replace JD-adherence as the anchor of performance.

02 · TDX — Talent Density Index

Density over headcount.

A single rolling number for what % of the org is genuinely top-tier in role. Drives hiring bar, comp envelope and exit decisions.

03 · AMO — Ability, Motivation, Opportunity

Engagement signal that points somewhere.

Survey engagement against the three levers managers can actually move — not vibes, not NPS.

04 · Enrichment vs Enlargement

Two different growth conversations.

Enrichment deepens craft in role. Enlargement adds scope. Mixing them is how people get promoted into the wrong job.
