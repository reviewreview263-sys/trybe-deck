TRYBE03 · The case study
— The case study

Rebuilding Kyra's performance system from a politeness machine into a signal engine.

Series A · ~60 people · ~$25M ARR · London. Inherited a JD-based biannual review process. Looked healthy on paper. Wasn't.

TRYBE04 · The contradiction
The contradiction

Two numbers on the same dashboard.
Telling opposite stories.

Legacy review
95%

Commercial reality
<60%

If everyone is meeting expectations, expectations aren't the right thing to measure.
TRYBE05 · Diagnosis
Diagnosis · Three faults in the legacy system

The review wasn't broken in execution.
It was broken in design.

01 · Anchor

Anchored to the JD, not impact.

Scored against role descriptions written at hire. People rated themselves "doing the job" — which they were. Commercial outcomes never entered the conversation.

02 · Cadence

Twice a year. Then forgotten.

Six-month gaps between signal and consequence. Managers couldn't remember February by August. Ratings drifted toward the mean by default.

03 · Distribution

No calibration. No curve.

Every manager rated their own. Without cross-team calibration, ratings inflated and comp drifted off signal. Talent density was invisible.

02 · Talent Density Index

Density over headcount.

TDX is the single rolling number for what percentage of the org is genuinely top-tier in role. It's the one metric I'd put on the board deck before any other people number. It drives the hiring bar, the comp envelope and exit decisions in one move.

At Kyra, TDX was the alignment device: every leader could argue about ratings — no one could argue about density.

TDX cycle 0
48%

Starting baseline. Defined by exec calibration on Impact Factor distribution.

TDX cycle 2
62%

One year in. Hiring bar lifted; two managed exits; three internal promotions on signal.

Target
70%

Density flywheel kicks in: top performers attract more top performers.

Floor
40%

Below 40% is the action threshold — quality & managed exits become priority.

TRYBE08 · 03 AMO · 04 Enrich/Enlarge
03 · AMO  ·  04 · Enrichment vs Enlargement

Engagement that points somewhere.
Growth split into two conversations.

03 · AMO

Ability · Motivation · Opportunity

Engagement isn't a vibe — it's three levers a manager can move.

04 · Enrich · Enlarge

Two growth tracks
Enrichment ↑
Deepen craft in role.

Higher mastery, harder problems, deeper specialism. Same scope, sharper edge. Most senior IC growth lives here.

↑ depth · mastery · staff IC
Enlargement →
Add scope.

More surface area, more people, broader ownership. The management ladder lives here — a different job, not a better one.

→ scope · breadth · management

Mixing the two is how people get promoted into the wrong job.
